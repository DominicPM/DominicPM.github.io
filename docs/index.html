Explicit support for foundational pluralism

Nearly all working mathematicians make extensive use of non-constructive, classical reasoning principles including the Law of the Excluded Middle and the Axiom of Choice in their day-to-day work. Interestingly, despite mostly working classically, mathematicians are able to “drop down” into restricted modes of reasoning — a purely-constructive mode, or a mode where Excluded Middle is permissible but the Axiom of Choice is not, for example — where and when the need arises.
